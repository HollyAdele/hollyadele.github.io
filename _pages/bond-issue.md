Do children need brand new schools to learn? No.   

Do brand new schools attract new families and good teachers? They do. 

My children will not benefit very much from these new buildings. Some of them attend the Dayton Regional STEM School (whose new building was a nonzero factor in my decision to send them), and the others will graduate before the projects are completed. I am going to vote yes from a property value perspective, which is as follows.

The best teachers can choose which school districts to work in. Kettering’s current reputation is that the community financially supports the schools. If Kettering gets the reputation that its community cannot afford to keep its schools safe, warm, and dry– that is a sinking ship that prospective teachers and families want to run from. 

Property values support the schools, and the schools support the property values. I wish that school funding wasn't tied to property value, but voting against this levy won't change that. There are many terrible things a homeowner can do to protect property values. Comparatively, building beautiful schools for our young people to prepare for the fast-moving future is a pretty good one.

We have many neighboring school districts with beautiful facilities.  The property tax increase is about the cost of a house project (that you don’t have to do any work on). New school buildings will keep prospective families interested in our neighborhoods, and our property values will stay high.
